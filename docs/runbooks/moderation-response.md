# Moderation Response

Use this runbook for launch-week and production moderation review of TailTag
reports, blocks, suspensions, and child safety concerns.

## Scope

TailTag allows user-generated profiles, fursuit listings, catch photos, catch
submissions, report text, and support text. TailTag is not a dating, hookup,
random chat, adult-content, or monetized UGC service. Adult, sexual,
pornographic, grooming, CSAM, CSAE, harassment, bullying, and illegal content or
conduct are prohibited.

## Report entry points

Users can report or block from these app surfaces:

| Surface | User control | Moderation destination |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public profile | Report profile, block user | Admin reports queue |
| Fursuit detail | Report fursuit, block owner | Admin reports queue |
| Catch detail | Report catch photo or fursuit, block owner | Admin reports queue |
| Pending catch photo | Report catch photo, block catcher | Admin reports queue |
| Settings | Blocked users management | User block list |
| Suspended account gate | Account suspension appeal email | Support inbox |
| Child Safety Standards page | Child safety report email | Support inbox |

## Ownership and cadence

- Finn owns launch-week moderation response unless another reviewer is assigned.
- Admin users with owner, organizer, or moderator access can review the reports
  queue.
- Before app store submission and launch week, confirm at least one staff/admin
  user can access the reports queue and player moderation panel.
- During launch week, review the reports queue at least daily.
- Treat child safety, CSAE/CSAM, severe harassment, credible harm, and other
  urgent safety reports as same-day reviews where possible.

## Triage

Review open reports from oldest to newest, escalating urgent safety reports first.

- Child safety, CSAE, or CSAM: treat as urgent, preserve relevant information,
  remove or restrict violating content where supported, suspend involved accounts
  when needed, and follow applicable reporting obligations.
- Harassment or bullying: review report details and related profile/fursuit/catch
  context, then warn, restrict, suspend, or dismiss with notes.
- Cheating or game abuse: review the reported user, fursuit, catch, and convention
  context, then resolve with corrective action or dismiss with notes.
- Spam, scams, or impersonation: restrict the account or content when confirmed,
  and record what was observed.
- Inappropriate, adult, sexual, or illegal content: remove or restrict content
  where supported, suspend accounts when needed, and document the policy basis.
- Other reports: classify the issue in resolution notes and take the least
  disruptive action that addresses the risk.

## Resolution workflow

1. Open the admin reports queue and inspect the report, reporter, target user,
   fursuit, catch context, and any linked player page.
2. Decide whether the report is valid, needs escalation, or should be dismissed.
3. Apply available moderation action when needed, such as account suspension or
   feature/content restriction.
4. Resolve or dismiss the report with factual resolution notes. Notes should
   summarize the observed issue, action taken, and escalation path if applicable.
5. Avoid copying unnecessary sensitive content into notes. Preserve only what is
   needed for safety, audit, and support.
6. For suspended users, direct appeals through the in-app suspension appeal email.
   For child safety concerns, use the child safety support path documented on the
   public Child Safety Standards page.

## Store review summary

TailTag provides in-app reporting for profiles, fursuits, and catch content, plus
blocking controls for users. Staff review reports in the admin reports queue,
record resolution notes, and can dismiss reports, restrict content or features
where supported, suspend accounts, and respond to child safety concerns through
the published support path. During launch week, the queue is reviewed daily, with
child safety and severe harm reports treated as urgent.
